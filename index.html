LASIK

Laser assisted in situ keratomileusis is a corneal flap based procedure for correction of refractive error in eye. It can be used in patients with myopia, hypermetropia and astigmatism at ease.

FEMTO LASIK

Blade-free LASIK Eye Surgery is a leap ahead of Traditional LASIK Surgery where a Femtosecond laser is used to create a precise flap. while in the Traditional blade LASIK technique, the flap is made by hand.

TOUCHLESS LASIK

This method is biomechanically superior to flap-based Lasik in lower refractive errors, and the surgery gets completed in a single step only. It is safe for patients with thin cornea and bilateral eyes are treated in the same session.

IPCL

Implantable Phakic Contact Lens is most suitable for the patients who cannot undergo LASIK or PRK due to thin corneal profile or extreme refractive error. IPCLs are surgically implanted directly into the eye.

FAQs

Most Frequent Questions And Answers

Lasik is the latest vision corrective surgery for a very delicate part of the eye. Millions of people have tried this latest procedure and corrected their vision within a few weeks. If you’re wearing glasses and contact lenses, you must be thinking about this surgery to get rid of glasses and contact lenses.

Patients normally prefer to have both eyes treated at one sitting, however, if the patient feels comfortable getting one eye treated at each session, this too can be done.

With the latest techniques and world-class equipment, LASIK eye surgery is considered to be one of the safest ways to get rid of glasses. But, like any surgical procedure, LASIK surgery too can pose certain side effects which include dry eyes, burning eyes, decreased visual clarity in dim light conditions, etc. However, these side effects are temporary and will eventually disappear as the eye heals.

Many of us think that summer is not the ideal season to get Lasik surgery done because of rising temperatures and humidity so probably most people avoid this season for any kind of surgery some believe that winters are the perfect time to get Lasik surgery done because of cooler temperatures. But, adding to this point that every season is fine for Lasik surgery but you really need to understand the post-surgery precautions deeply. So, whether it is summer or winter both are ideal seasons for the Lasik procedure.

LASIK is a day care procedure and therefore, post-surgery you are allowed to go home on the same day. Antibiotic and anti-inflammatory drops are administered along with temporary padding over the eyes so as to avoid any kind of eye infection. The whole procedure takes around 30 minutes only. Visual recovery may vary from a few hours to a few days until it gets stabilized. However, you will be allowed to return to your normal activities within 2-3 days following surgery. Follow-up and aftercare visits are to be adhered strictly as per your surgeon’s directions.

A second, or enhancement procedure can be performed to provide additional correction, especially if the objective of the surgery is not met during the first time. However, most people do not require additional surgery.
